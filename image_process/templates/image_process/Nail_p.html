What is Nail Psoriasis?

Nail psoriasis, also known as psoriatic onychodystrophy, is a chronic inflammatory condition affecting the nails, often associated with psoriasis. It can manifest in various forms and is typically linked to underlying psoriatic disease, which may include skin lesions and psoriatic arthritis.

Nail psoriasis is characterized by inflammation of the nail matrix and nail bed, leading to distinct clinical changes such as discoloration, pitting, and structural alterations of the nails. It affects a significant number of individuals with psoriasis, with estimates suggesting that up to 90% of patients with chronic plaque psoriasis will experience nail involvement at some point in their lives23.

Types of Nail Psoriasis

1. Nail Matrix Involvement

This type affects the area where the nail grows and can lead to:

Nail Pitting: Small depressions on the surface of the nail due to abnormal keratinization.

Leukonychia: White spots or lines on the nails caused by damage to the nail matrix.

Red Spots in the Lunula: Red discoloration at the base of the nail, indicating inflammation23.

2. Nail Bed Involvement

This type impacts the area beneath the nail plate, resulting in:

Onycholysis: Separation of the nail from the nail bed, often leading to discomfort.

Subungual Hyperkeratosis: Thickening of skin beneath the nail, which can cause pain and difficulty in nail care.

Oil Drop Discoloration: A characteristic yellow-orange spot under the nail that resembles a drop of oil14.

3. Hyponychium and Nail Fold Involvement

This type affects areas surrounding the nails:

Splinter Hemorrhages: Tiny blood spots that appear under the nails due to capillary damage.

Paronychia: Inflammation of the skin around the nails, which can lead to pain and swelling.

Acropustulosis: Pustular lesions that can occur at the tips of fingers or toes56.

4. Combined Nail Involvement

In many cases, patients may experience a combination of symptoms from both matrix and bed involvement, leading to more complex presentations.

When to Consult a Specialist?

Consultation with a dermatologist or healthcare provider is recommended when:

There are visible changes in your nails that resemble symptoms of nail psoriasis (e.g., pitting, discoloration).

Symptoms are accompanied by pain or discomfort affecting daily activities.

There is a family history of psoriasis or psoriatic arthritis.

You have existing psoriasis on your skin or joints and notice changes in your nails.

Symptoms do not improve with over-the-counter treatments or home remedies345.

Early diagnosis and management are crucial since nail psoriasis can be indicative of more severe underlying psoriatic disease and may correlate with an increased risk of developing psoriatic arthritis. Treatment options vary depending on severity but may include topical treatments, systemic medications, or phototherapy.
